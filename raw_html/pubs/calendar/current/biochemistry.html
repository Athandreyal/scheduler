"b'\t\t\t\t\t\tBiochemistry 'b'\t\t\t\t\t\t341      'b'\t\t\t\t\t"b'\t\t\t\t\t\tBiochemistry of Life Processes'b'\t\t\t\t\t
'b'\t\t
'b'\t\t\tEmphasis is placed on describing the chemistry of biochemical molecules including proteins, carbohydrates, lipids, and nucleic acids, and how this relates to cell structure and life processes. Basic concepts of metabolism are introduced, focusing on the breakdown of carbohydrates for energy. The laboratory component reinforces learning of the lecture material, while teaching technical skills and the analysis and interpretation of experiments involving biochemical molecules.'b''b'\t\t\t
Course Hours:'b''b'\t\t\t3 units; (3-3/2)'b''b'\t\t\t
Prerequisite(s):'b''b'\t\t\tChemistry 351.'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t
Antirequisite(s):'b'\t\t\tNot open to majors in the Department of Biological Sciences or Natural Sciences concentrators in Biological Sciences. Credit for Biochemistry 341 and 393 will not be allowed. 'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t'b''b'\t\t\t

'b'\t\t\tBiomolecules and metabolism as a foundation for understanding molecular organization and reactions of life. 'b'\t\t

'b''b'\t\t\t
